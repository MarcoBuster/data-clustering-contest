'They chose life': GOP congressman tells story of how his staffer was almost aborted

15 Nov 2019, 00:14 by Tim Pearce

Rep. Chip Roy relayed a story about one of his staffers as evidence that abortion can kill a life that otherwise would have been "excellent."

The Texas Republican used part of his time during a House hearing on state abortion laws on Thursday to tell the story of one of his staffers, Jonah. Jonah’s parents dismissed their doctor’s recommendation that they abort him and his twin during pregnancy.

"In the winter of 1996, a couple went in for a checkup. They were excited. They were recently informed they had twins," the 47-year-old lawmaker said.

The routine checkup turned worrisome after the doctor conducted more and more tests, he said. The doctor diagnosed the unborn twins with cystic fibrosis, a potentially fatal disease that affects the lungs and digestive system. The doctor said the babies would likely die just hours after birth.

"'I would recommend termination,' said the doctor. The couple said the first thing that came into their mind, 'No,' and walked out. They chose life," Roy said.

"Those twins would grow up to become excellent men. I know this because Jonah works for me. Right here. He is one of my staffers," Roy said.
