October’s inflation in Latvia higher than average in EU and Eurozone

15 Nov 2019 by BNN

In October 2019, inflation in Latvia was higher than the average in the European Union and Eurozone, according to information published by Eurostat on Friday, 15 November.

Annual inflation in Latvia was 2.2% in October. Inflation was higher in Romania (3.2%), Hungary (3%), Slovakia (2.9%), Netherlands (2.8%), Czech Republic (2.6%) and Poland (2.3%). Latvia is followed by Sweden and Bulgaria (1.6% in both), Britain, Slovenia and Lithuania (1.5% in all), as well as Malta and Estonia (1.4% in both).

When compared to the same month of 2018, inflation in October 2019 was registered in all EU member states except for Cyprus, Greece and Portugal, where consumer prices have dropped by 0.5%, 0.3% and 0.1% respectively. In Austria consumer prices have increased 1%, in Finland, France and Germany – by 0.9%, in Luxembourg price increase was 0.8%, followed by Croatia, Ireland and Denmark (0.6% in all three), as well as Italy, Spain and Belgium (0.2% in all three).

Average inflation in the EU was 1.1% in October. Eurozone’s inflation was 0.7%.

When compared to September, consumer prices in October both in the EU and Eurozone have increased 0.1%.

When compared to September in Latvia and Lithuania prices have increased 0.1% in October, whereas in Estonia prices have dropped 0.2%. The highest inflation of October was registered in Spain (0.7%), Hungary and Netherlands (0.6% in both), as well as Czech Republic and Denmark (0.5% in both), whereas the highest deflation was registered in Malta (-1.4%), as well as Portugal and Cyprus (-0.4%).
