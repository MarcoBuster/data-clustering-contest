Czech Republic, England, France and Turkey have booked a spot alongside Belgium, Italy, Russia, Poland, Ukraine and Spain at UEFA Euro 2020.

England qualified for the finals on Thursday, with a 7-0 victory at home to Montenegro and the Czech Republic joined them with a 2-0 victory over Kosovo.

Turkey secured their place in the finals draw with a 0-0 against Iceland at home on Thursday, while France beat Moldova 2-1.

The top two teams, twenty in total will qualify automatically for the competition.

Four more nations will qualify via playoffs, qualification for which is based on UEFA Nations League performance.

The draw will be held on Saturday, November 30 in Bucharest, Romania.
