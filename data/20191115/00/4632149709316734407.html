Things to do today in Bengaluru

15 Nov 2019, 00:30 by Bangalore Mirror Bureau

Sound of music
The Flights of Fantasy music festival is back, this time featuring a performance by Vishaka Hari. She is a Carnatic music vocalist and proponent of Harikatha, otherwise known as a storyteller of ‘Kathakalakshepam’. This year, she has chosen ‘Moksha through Bhakti’ as the subject of recital for her show.

WHERE: Chowdiah Memorial Hall, 16th Cross Road, Malleshwaram
WHEN: 6.30pm onwards, November 15
CALL: 4667180

Get up and dance
DJ Devansh is back to the console at this rooftop destination. His commercial success comes from the electro house, progressive house and big room sets that he mixes up so seamlessly, making sure you stay glued to the dance floor. So get your buddies, dust off those dancing shoes and come over, bring the roof down.

WHERE: High Ultra Lounge, Rooftop, World Trade Centre, Malleshwaram
WHEN: 9pm onwards, November 15
CALL: 8045674567

Kids will love it
On Day 2, the organisers of ‘Kids’ Day Out Film Festival’ have lined up Aladdin, and Incredibles-2 for screening. Honestly, neither of the films need introduction, but if you are late on the scene, know this. Aladdin is the story of a lovable street urchin who meets princess Jasmine at her exotic palace, where he also runs into a magic oil lamp and a wisecracking genie hidden inside it. As for Incredibles-2... Entrusted with a task to restore the public’s faith in the superheroes, Helen sets off on a mission to catch a super villain while Bob faces the challenges of a stay-at-home parent.

WHERE: PVR Phoenix Marketcity, PVR Forum,
PVR Superplex Vega WHEN: 4pm and 7pm
onwards, November 15 CALL: 9845706155

He dares you not to laugh
Jeeveshu Ahluwalia is all set to make your Friday outrageously funny. His sense of humour comes from observations, everyday conversations, quirky monologues, peculiar situations and some eccentric friends. He is frequently seen on comedy shows and appears in events at auditoriums, clubs, colleges and corporate functions throughout the country.

WHERE: Phoenix Marketcity, White-field Main Road, Mahadevapura
WHEN: 7.30pm, November 15
ENTRY: `499, bookmyshow.com

Attend a film festival
Film Bengaluru International Film Festival (FBIFF) will open in the city today with the screening of Mumukshu, Kamaru, Anamadheya Appayamma, Salam, A Coin Tale, Ashmina, etc. Since the three-day festival focuses on the theme ‘Kindness Matters’, on Day 1, panel discussions will be held on the topics of ‘kindness in films’, ‘ethics in filmmaking’, ‘bringing social and positive impact in the society through responsible filmmaking’. More films will follow over the weekend.

WHERE: UB City Amphitheatre, Vittal Mallya Road
WHEN: 5pm–9.30pm, November 15 DETAILS: filmbengaluru.com

The saree story

Owning an exquisite Kanjivaram saree is a dream for many women, if not all. But why wish, when you can make it come true? Check out ‘The Exhibition and Sale of Vault Heirloom Kanjivaram’, which is bringing to town a range of exclusive sarees, each one laboriously handwoven by Kanchipuram’s master weavers. It’s an effort to revive ancient designs and weaving techniques.

WHERE: Kanakavalli, #5, Setlur Street, Langford Town
WHEN: 10am-8pm, November 15

CALL: 9940515627
