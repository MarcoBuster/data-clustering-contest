College Football Playoff Rankings: LSU Moves To No. 1 After Beating Alabama In Biggest Bet Game Of Season

15 Nov 2019 by Jay Ginsbach

The biggest game of the college football season lived up to the hype. Alabama (-6) couldn’t live up to the favortism however, as LSU went into Tuscaloosa and came away with a most impressive and deserving win, 46-41. The victory moved the LSU Tigers to No. 1 in this week’s college football playoff poll, and dropped Alabama to No. 5 and on the outside looking in for the four team college football playoff.

  1. LSU (9-0)
  2. Ohio State (9-0)
  3. Clemson (10-0)
  4. Georgia (8-1)
  5. Alabama (8-1)
  6. Oregon (8-1)
  7. Utah (8-1)
  8. Minnesota (9-0)
  9. Penn State (8-1)
  10. Oklahoma (8-1)

The betting market was all-in for the Alabama - LSU game, and most bettors cashed in with LSU including on the moneyline. Late last week, FanDuel sportsbook reported that 72% of bets and 58% of the money was on the LSU Tigers. FanDuel and every sportsbook was already profitable on Saturday by the time LSU and Alabama kicked off, as Penn State (-6.5) was upset by Minnesota 31-26 early in the day. Late in the week, 86% of bets and 87% of the money was on the Penn State Nittany Lions at FanDuel and most sportsbooks has one-sided action supporting the Nittany Lions.

Caesars sportsbook in Las Vegas reported that the LSU-Alabama had more money bet on the game than was bet on any Sunday NFL Week 11 game.

LSU coach Ed Orgeron is trying to keep his team focused on the next game with practice, preparation and production. The Tigers travel to play Ole Miss this week with LSU a 21-point favorite but up to 21.5 at William Hill sportsbooks; America’s largest sportsbook operator.

LSU finishes the regular season at home against Arkansas (2-8) and Texas A&M (6-3) before a likely SEC Championship game against Georgia in Atlanta. One thing for certain is that even a one-loss LSU team will make the college football playoff. LSU has four wins over teams who were ranked in the AP Top 10 when the Tigers faced them.  

Senior quarterback Joe Burrow completed 31 of 39 passes for 393 yards and 3 touchdowns to lead LSU to victory over Alabama. Burrow is now the heavy favorite to win the Heisman Trophy at -1000 at FanDuel sportsbook. The next closest Heisman contender is Ohio State quarterback Justin Fields, who is +1000 at DraftKings sportsbook and +2000 (20-1) at FanDuel. However, liability and amount wagered on players for the Heisman Trophy and other props varies, thus odds are different at various sportsbooks. Caesars sportsbook properties has Burrow (-700) the favorite followed by a pair of 7-1 shots in Fields (+700) and Oklahoma quarterback Jalen Hurts (+700).

Other Top 5 News, Schedules and Saturday Betting Data

Ohio State made headlines this week as the biggest favorite in Big Ten history when they play at Rutgers as a 52.5 point favorite. At William Hill sportsbooks across the country, 94% of the money is on the Buckeye’s laying more than 50 points. Ohio State still plays No. 9 Penn State and No. 15 Michigan before a potential Big Ten championship game. A win at Rutgers followed by a home win over Penn State would secure a spot for Ohio State in the Big Ten championship game regardless of the result at rival Michigan. However, the Buckeye’s are playing for more than just a Big Ten title as one of the favorites to reach the college football playoff.

No. 3 Clemson is a 34-point favorite at home this week against Wake Forest. The Tigers have a bye next week before the regular season finale at state-rival South Carolina. The ACC championship game follows against Virginia.

The biggest game this Saturday is No. 4 Georgia at No. 12 Auburn, and Georgia is a 2.5-point favorite with 63% of the spread bets and 71% of the money on Georgia (-2.5) at William Hill sportsbooks. A win by Georgia this week or next week at Texas A&M secures the Bulldogs spot in the SEC championship game. Auburn and Alabama still have their huge rivalry game Nov. 30 at Auburn.

Minnesota Makes Move Into Top 10

The Minnesota Golden Gophers upset Penn State 31-26 for the programs biggest win in decades. That was another big result in favor of the sportsbooks, as a majority of the bets and money was on Penn State (-6.5). The victory moved undefeated Minnesota (9-0) to No. 8 in the CFP poll and the Gophers made the biggest move after ranking No. 17 in the the opening college football playoff poll last week.

The Gophers are still a big longshot to make the college football playoff, but a trip to the Rose Bowl for the first time since 1962 is well within reach for Minnesota. The Gophers are a 3-point underdog this week at Iowa, where they have not won since 1999. But a victory over the Hawkeye’s and next week at Northwestern would secure the Big Ten West title and put the Gophers into the Big Ten championship game. Regardless of wins or losses the next two weeks, a victory over No. 14 Wisconsin in the season finale Nov. 30 would also put Minnesota into the Big Ten championship game where even a loss likely sends the Gophers to the Rose Bowl against the Pac-12 champion.

No. 6 Oregon and No. 7 Utah are on a collision course to play in the Pac-12 championship game as long as Utah wins their final three games as favorite or USC loses once. Utah is a 21.5-point favorite over UCLA this Saturday while Oregon is a 27-point favorite over Arizona with both teams playing at home.

The Pac-12 conference currently sends the winner of the Pac-12 championship game to the Rose Bowl or College Football playoffs. The Alamo Bowl has the second pick of teams after the Rose Bowl and/or New Year's Six games are decided.

When is the 2019 College Football Playoff semifinals?

The CFP semifinals are set for Dec. 28, 2019. One semifinal is the Peach Bowl from Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta. The other semifinal is the Fiesta Bowl in Glendale, Arizona.

When is the 2020 College Football Playoff national championship?

The CFP national championship is Jan. 13, 2020 at the Mercedes-Benz Superdome in New Orleans.
