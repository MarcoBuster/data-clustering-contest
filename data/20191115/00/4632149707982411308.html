A day after deadly floods, Italy declares emergency in Venice

15 Nov 2019, 00:30 by AFP

VENICE Italy declared a state of emergency for Venice Tuesday after an exceptional tide surged through churches, shops and homes, causing millions of euros worth of damage to the UNESCO city.

Tourists larked around in the flooded St Mark’s Square in the sunshine, snapping selfies in their neon plastic boots and taking advantage of a respite in bad weather which has driven the high tides.


Prime Minister Giuseppe Conte, who has called the flooding “a blow to the heart of our country”, met Venice’s mayor and emergency services before visiting the places hit by the from the highest tide in 50 years. Several museums remained closed to the public on Thursday.
