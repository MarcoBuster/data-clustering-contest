7 Hotels Around The World Serving Thanksgiving Dinner

15 Nov 2019 by Ali Wunderman

There’s a lot of pressure in putting together a delicious multi-course meal that’s only served once a year, so this Thanksgiving, give thanks to all the hotels around the world serving up this annual feast so you don’t have to. Whether you’re already out exploring the world, or just looking for an excuse to, here are seven hotels across the globe prepared to overload your plate with turkey, stuffing, and all the other Thanksgiving favorites. And the best part is, you don’t have to clean any of it up after.  

W Punta de Mita, Riviera Nayarit, Mexico

Between November 28th (Thanksgiving Day) and December 2nd, guests of the W Punta de Mita can indulge in their seasonal food series, A Taste of Autumn. The program is managed by Montreal-based Chef Kimberly Lallouz of Miss Prêt à Manger and Monsieur Restaurant and Bar fame. Events range from a traditional Thanksgiving dinner on the 28th from 8-10pm in their Venazu restaurant, to live cooking demos at Mesa1, the resort’s most exclusive dining setting: a giant outdoor table carved from an ancient tree that offers just one nightly seating for up to 12 guests. The dining experience is part of an accommodation deal with the hotel, and begins at $676 per night. 

The Curtain, London, England

Americans traveling in England should look no further than The Curtain Hotel in Shoreditch for their annual culinary indulgence. Their restaurant, Red Rooster Shoreditch, serves up American cuisine with its roots in New York’s Harlem neighborhood. Chef Marcus Samuelsson will be serving a 3-course Thanksgiving menu fusing American Southern cuisine with his own cosmopolitan sensibilities. For £55 ($67) per person, guests can choose from classics like Roast Turkey, Stone Bass, or Yellowtail Ceviche, then finish with Pumpkin Doughnuts with bourbon ice-cream. And for an extra dose of Americana, a NOLA-style brass band will be playing live for diners. Reserve a table with the hotel.

The Bowery Hotel, New York City, USA

It doesn’t take leaving the country to enjoy a Thanksgiving dinner without having to prepare or clean up after it. New Yorkers can head to Gemma at their very own Bowery Hotel, where Executive Chef Andrea Taormina will be serving up a traditional Thanksgiving feast with an Italian twist. Menu highlights includes Fall Squash Soup, served with sage, mascarpone, roasted pumpkin seeds, roasted turkey with black truffle gravy, porcini and chestnut stuffing, roasted brussels sprouts, and sweet potato mash, followed by pumpkin cheesecake sporting an amaretto cookie crust. It costs $75 per person ,and reservations can be made by calling (212) 505-7300.

San Ignacio Resort Hotel, San Ignacio, Belize

There’s a Thanksgiving special taking place at the San Ignacio Resort Hotel on the edge of the jungle in Belize that American travelers will love. Chef Enrique Awe of the hotel’s famous Running W Restaurant will be serving up herb-roasted turkey, pineapple glazed ham, and carved garlic and herb-crusted tri tip alongside sweet potato casserole, mashed potatoes, and other traditional trimmings, starters, and desserts. Live music will be played alongside this buffet-style dinner, which costs $60BZD ($30 USD). Reservations are recommended and can be made in advance directly with the hotel.

Heaven Restaurant and Hotel, Kigali, Rwanda

Rwanda may be a fairly long distance from the U.S., but that isn’t stopping Heaven Restaurant and Hotel from putting together a Thanksgiving feast. While overlooking Kigali, guests will dine on a traditional Thanksgiving buffet, sip wine and cocktails, and listen to a live performance by a local musician. The meal takes place throughout the evening, from 4:30-9:30pm, and costs 35,000 RWF ($38 USD). Reservations are required to take part in this celebration. 

Koloa Landing Resort, Kauai, USA

It may not feel like Thanksgiving when it’s 80 degrees out and your days are spent lounging on the beach or at the pool, but Koloa Landing Resort’s Thanksgiving buffet brunch will be just as festive as any mainland celebration. From 11am-2pm in the hotel’s ballroom, guests will enjoy classics roasted turkey with homemade gravy and smoked bacon mac and cheese, but Hawaiian fare as well, including a raw bar and Chef Sam Choy’s famous ahi and tako poke. The feast costs $65 per person, and $29 for children under 10, and while it’s not limited to hotel guests, reservations should be made in advance. 

Four Seasons Hotel Tokyo at Marunouchi, Tokyo, Japan

Thanksgiving doesn’t get much more luxurious than the multi-day feast offered this year at the Four Seasons Hotel Tokyo at Marunouchi. Beginning November 22nd and ending Thanksgiving day, a special lunch and dinner will be available at the hotel’s Motif Restaurant and Bar. For lunch, which costs ¥6,500 ($60 USD), the menu includes amuse and dessert buffets alongside turkey with gravy and seasonal vegetables. Dinner costs ¥9,000 ($83 USD) and serves up pumpkin soup, onion tarts, and salmon in addition to the turkey entree.
