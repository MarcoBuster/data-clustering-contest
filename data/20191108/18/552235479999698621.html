Leeann Dempster defends Hibs summer recruitment and hits out at 'unnecessary criticism'

The Hibs chief executive defended the club's transfer work in the summer.

08 Nov 2019, 18:17 by Jonathan McFarlane

Hibs chief executive Leeann Dempster has hit out against claims the club's transfer policy has resulted in the sacking of Paul Heckingbottom.

The Yorkshireman was sacked after a 5-2 semi-final defeat by Celtic at Hampden topped a poor run of Premiership form.

He made an excellent start to life in Edinburgh and managed to orchestrate a revival that saw the club claim an unlikely top six finish.

But his nine summer signings, many from England's lower reaches, have made little impact and Hibs sit a solitary point from bottom.

The narrative has taken hold that the manager, undoubtedly a talented coach, underestimated our game and signed a raft of stars unable to cope with top level demands.

Dempster bristles at such suggestions and has backed the players to show their worth in the season ahead.

She said: "I don't think anything went wrong with it to be honest.

"We have a strong group of players here. We need to make some tweaks around the edges.
