Funeral for much-loved teacher Mrs Clarke as Plymouth says goodbye

The funeral dates for popular Devonport High School For Girls teacher Helen Clarke have been announced

08 Nov 2019, 18:36 by Sarah Waddington

The funeral dates for a popular and much-loved Plymouth teacher have been announced.

Helen Clarke died on October 25 at Derriford Hospital following a battle with cancer. She leaves behind her husband Richard, children Stephen, Deborah and Simon, and grandchildren Joseph, Lily, Lois, Matthew, Abigail and Hope.

Tributes poured in for the 'wonderful' former form tutor and physics teacher at Devonport High School for Girls following her death.

Now Mrs Clarke's family have announced her funeral arrangements.

A funeral announcement says: 'Called home to her eternal rest on 25th October 2019, while at Derriford Hospital.

"With Christ, which is far better"

Beloved wife of Richard, mother of Stephen, Deborah and Simon, mother-in-law of Emma, Timothy and Jacinth and grandmother of Joseph, Lily, Lois, Matthew, Abigail and Hope.

Thanksgiving Service to be held on Friday 22nd November at 12 noon at Beacon Park Baptist Church, Peverell, PL3 4JY and afterwards in the Church Hall.

Either flowers, or preferably donations, if desired, made payable to Care Sri Lanka may be left as a retiring collection, online wcpltd.com or sent to

Walter C. Parson

Crownhill, PL6 5DY

Tel: 01752 767676

"Absent from the body, ...present with the Lord".'

Hundreds of thoughtful tribute messages from former students and members of the community have been made for Mrs Clarke.

Mrs Clarke had taught Physics at the grammar school for 17 years and was well-known by hundreds of students past and present.

The school say staff were very sad to hear the news and Mrs Clarke will be 'greatly missed by all'.

Headteacher of Devonport High School for Girls, Mrs Anita Hemsi said: "We were very sad to hear the news that Mrs Helen Clarke, who had been a dedicated and inspiring Physics teacher for over 17 years at Devonport High School Girls, passed away at the end of last week.

"Helen taught at the school for many years and was an active member of the Christian Union group at the school as well as being a key member of her local church. She will be greatly missed by all. Our thoughts and prayers are with her family and friends."
